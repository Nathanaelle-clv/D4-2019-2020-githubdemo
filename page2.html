By pollinating from flower to flower to suck their sweet juice, called nectar, pollinating insects, such as bees and bumblebees, without even noticing it, carry pollen to the pistil of neighboring flowers of the same species. In some cases, the flower inclines its stamens on the back of the insect that has landed on it, thus promoting the transport of pollen on other flowers.
